# Value Proposition - Clarified 2026-02-09

## The Core Insight

These aren't generic ChatGPT responses with persona prompts slapped on top. These are **highly curated, battle-tested personas** that respond with distinct perspectives matching real population segments.

## What Makes This Different

### What we're NOT

- ❌ Generic AI with a persona description
- ❌ ChatGPT that you have to prompt-engineer yourself
- ❌ Inconsistent responses that feel robotic
- ❌ DIY persona building that takes weeks to get right

### What we ARE

- ✅ **Pre-built, proven personas** - already tested and refined
- ✅ **Distinct perspectives** - each one thinks and responds differently (not just different words, different viewpoints)
- ✅ **Rich internal context** - they don't just have a job title, they have motivations, constraints, team dynamics, and personal circumstances that shape their responses
- ✅ **Behaviorally accurate** - they respond like real people in that segment actually would, because they're thinking from within realistic life circumstances
- ✅ **Ready to use** - no setup, no prompt crafting, no testing phase
- ✅ **Curated roster** - we've done the hard work of building realistic, useful personas

## The Work We've Already Done For You

1. **Deep persona development** - Not just demographics, but rich internal context:

   - Personal motivations (e.g., up for promotion, building credibility)
   - Organizational dynamics (boss, reports, stakeholders)
   - Constraints and pressures (budget, timeline, politics)
   - Past experiences that shape their worldview

2. **Testing & refinement** - Iterated until responses feel authentic, not robotic

   - Real people respond from within their life context, not in a vacuum
   - Each persona has specific circumstances that influence their perspective

   - Not just what they say, but how they think through decisions

4. **Consistency** - Each persona maintains their perspective across conversations
   - Same motivations, same constraints, same thought patterns

## The Depth That Makes It Real

**Why generic ChatGPT prompts don't work:**

Prompt: "Act like a product manager and review this deck"

- ❌ Generic, surface-level feedback
- ❌ No context about their specific situation
- ❌ Inconsistent voice and perspective
- ❌ Feels like AI trying to roleplay

**Why our personas are different:**

Maya isn't a prompt - she's a fully realized person:

- She works at a specific type of company (large enterprise SaaS)
- She manages 3 reports with different skill levels
- Her boss values strategic thinking and low-risk execution
- She's up for promotion in July, so she's thinking about visibility
- She's navigated failed product launches before and learned from them
- She has political capital to spend and needs to choose battles wisely

When Maya reviews your deck, she's not just giving "PM feedback" - she's responding from within this rich context. That's what makes it feel real and useful.

## Why This Matters

You're not testing your idea on:

- Generic AI that agrees with everything
- Colleagues who are too close to the problem
- Expensive focus groups that take weeks to schedule

You're getting **instant feedback from perspectives that match your actual target customers** - including their motivations, constraints, and real-world context - without the cost, time, or brand risk of real user research.

## Better Terminology

Instead of "voice" (feels too abstract/marketing-y), use:

- **Perspective** - "get feedback from 3 different perspectives"
- **Take** - "see Maya's take on your deck" (casual, modern)
- **Point of view** - "each persona brings a distinct POV"
- **Lens** - "view your campaign through different lenses"

Recommend: **"perspective"** for formal copy, **"take"** for casual/conversational copy.

## CTA Guidelines - Critical!

**The product is about USING twins, not BUILDING them.**

### ❌ Bad CTAs (imply creation/building)

- "Build your twin"
- "Create your persona"
- "Start building"
- "Design your customer"
- "Get started" (too vague)

### ✅ Good CTAs (focus on interaction/usage)

- "Start getting feedback"
- "Talk to the roster"
- "Message Maya"
- "Ask a question"
- "Get their take"
- "Try it now"
- "Email a twin"
- "Get feedback in seconds"

### Why This Matters

The entire value prop is: **we've already built these for you**. If CTAs focus on building, it contradicts the core message and adds perceived friction.

The mental model should be:

1. Browse the roster (like browsing a team)
2. Pick the perspectives you need
3. Start asking questions immediately

NOT:

1. ~~Define your customer~~
2. ~~Build a persona~~
3. ~~Configure responses~~

### CTA Clarity - Where Does It Actually Go?

**Important principle:** The CTA should be clear about what it actually does, not just sound good.

**Current hero CTA:** Links to #pricing (payment section)

- ❌ Vague: "Get started," "Try it now" (doesn't clarify it's a purchase)
- ❌ Oversells: "Talk to your customers" (that happens AFTER purchase, not when you click)
- ✅ Clear: "Book your 3 personas," "Choose your plan," "See pricing"

**The CTA should match the destination:**

- If it links to pricing → be clear it's about purchasing
- If it links to a demo → say "Try the demo" or "Talk to Maya"
- If it links to signup → say "Create account" or "Sign up"
